Design Process

1. Concept & Storytelling

We held a meeting to set the concept and story, which is the first order of the design.

arrow_downward

2. Selection of Motif

We selected a motif that fits the concept.

arrow_downward

3. IdeaSketch

Our team did an idea sketch to decide on the design.

arrow_downward

4. 2D Design

The furniture design blueprint was created using the AutoCAD program. Based on the furniture sketch, dimensions were accurately measured and expressed in a two-dimensional format.

arrow_downward

5. Modeling Process

3D models of the furniture were created using the Rhino program. This was used to check the feasibility of implementation.

arrow_downward

6. Material Representation

To add realism to the completed 3D model, the KeyShot program was used for material representation.
This allowed us to visually confirm how the furniture would look after completion.

Production Process

1. Disassembly Stage

First, the existing chair was disassembled using a saw.

arrow_downward

2. Additional Parts Production Stage

Required additional parts were manufactured using CNC machining technology.

arrow_downward

3. Sanding Stage

The surface coatings on the disassembled parts were removed using sandpaper.
Newly created parts were also sanded to achieve a smooth finish.

arrow_downward

4. Assembly Stage

The furniture was assembled using wood glue and fixing tools.

arrow_downward

5. Varnishing Stage

Multiple layers of varnish were applied as a finishing touch to the assembled furniture.
Sanding was repeated before and after varnishing to achieve a smooth surface.

arrow_downward

6. Additional Work

Additional components were created outside of the main furniture production process.

  • Mat: Materials were purchased separately to create a mat that matched the chair.
  • Mobile: Initially planned for self-production, but due to time constraints, a purchased product was used.
